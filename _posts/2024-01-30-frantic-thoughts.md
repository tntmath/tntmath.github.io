Hhhhh

&emsp; It feels extremely distressing. I am experiencing a profound state of analysis paralysis. I feel like whatever I touched, I destroyed it. I looked around, cannot help but feel a sense of sadness and pity for myself. Why have things gotten so bad lately?

&emsp; People go through depression, people got better in just one or two days, how can they do that? I guess when they have a permanent job, the fear is somewhat lessened. The challenge I consistently face involves comparing, observing, and subsequently criticizing myself. They said “insanity is doing things exactly the same but expecting different results”, well, I think I am insane. 

&emsp; I could not figure out why life has been so rough lately. Sometimes calming down, thinking, in the end, does it matter? Missing deadlines, neglecting projects, putting them on top of health, or time for the people I love, at the end, do they matter? I mean, deep down, I probably still care about a future where the location I end up in might have a significant impact on my thinking., so I guess it matters to me, in a way that I bitterly accepted. 

&emsp; I have experienced a decline in my critical thinking skills; my attention span has become short, and my concentration is now limited. Looking around, I don’t see the sense of belonging as well. A couple of years devoted into something I was not even sure, what was I chasing in the end? I always thought I was chasing stability, making myself as good as Robert Lee, the legendary general to make me feel better. It turns out completely funny, I was chasing something I did not even know how to define, sometime I did not chase anything at all, just lay there, wait and wait. But then that feeling got old pretty quickly and I got bored, I started to chase something again, and never being consistent. I thought I have found a way, but no, I am still lost. 

&emsp; Enough rambling, perhaps sleep, a remedy beyond my grasp, is the missing link.
