Comparison Principles - Notes and Remarks

tntmath

2025/08/02

We consider the generalized Dirichlet boundary value problem:

\begin{equation}\label{eq:DirichletBoundaryProblem} \begin{cases} \begin{aligned} \lambda u + |Du|^p - f(x) - \varepsilon\Delta u &= 0 &&\qquad\text{in}\quad \Omega, \\ u &= g &&\qquad \text{on}\quad \partial\Omega. \end{aligned} \end{cases} \end{equation}

The paper [1] discusses a comparison principle for the generalized Dirichlet problem in the time-dependent setting, dealing with Hamiltonians of the form \( H(x, \xi) = |\xi|^p - f(x) \), where \( p > 2 \).

This result is also mentioned and restated in [2], where the static version \( \eqref{eq:DirichletBoundaryProblem} \) is presented, and the time-independent case is reduced to the time-dependent case, by result in Theorem 3.1 in [1].

References

  1. G. Barles and F. Da Lio, On the generalized Dirichlet problem for viscous Hamilton–Jacobi equations, Journal de Mathématiques Pures et Appliquées, vol. 83, no. 1, pp. 53–75, 2004. doi:10.1016/S0021-7824(03)00070-9
  2. T. Tabet Tchamba, Large time behavior of solutions of viscous Hamilton–Jacobi equations with superquadratic Hamiltonian, Asymptotic Analysis, vol. 66, no. 3-4, pp. 161–186, 2010. doi:10.3233/ASY-2009-0965
